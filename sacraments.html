The Seven Sacraments

A sacrament is a visible form of invisible grace—God's gift to us for our salvation and spiritual growth.

Baptism

Baptism is the sacrament of new birth in Christ. Through baptism, we are reborn into God's family and begin our journey of faith.

Learn more →

Confirmation

Confirmation is the sacrament of spiritual strength. Like the Apostles who received the Holy Spirit at Pentecost, the confirmed are sealed with the gifts of the Holy Spirit to live courageously as Christ's witnesses.

Learn more →

Eucharist

The Eucharist is the sacrament of Christ's presence. In receiving the Body and Blood of Christ, we are nourished spiritually and united as one body in the Church.

Learn more →

Penance & Reconciliation

In the sacrament of Penance, we receive God's healing and forgiveness. Confession is available Saturdays from 4:00-4:50 p.m., and by appointment on other days.

Learn more →

Anointing of the Sick

The Anointing of the Sick brings spiritual and physical comfort to those suffering illness or facing the end of life. This sacrament restores hope and strength through God's grace.

Learn more →

Holy Orders

Holy Orders is the sacrament through which Christ's mission is carried on in the Church through bishops, priests, and deacons.

Learn more →

Matrimony

Matrimony is the sacrament by which a man and woman unite their lives in a permanent covenant of love, reflecting Christ's love for the Church.

Learn more →
