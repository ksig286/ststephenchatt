Sacraments

Sacred encounters with God's grace

The sacraments are sacred signs and means of grace that connect us to Christ and strengthen our faith community.

Baptism

Baptism is the sacrament of initiation. Through Baptism, we become children of God and members of the Church. Whether you have questions about infant baptism or are interested in adult baptism, our parish team is here to guide you.

Questions? Contact us to schedule a consultation.

Confirmation

Confirmation strengthens the gifts received in Baptism. Young people and adults deepen their commitment to Christ and receive the Holy Spirit's grace to live as faithful Catholics. Registration for Confirmation preparation takes place in the fall.

Learn more or register: Contact our office

Eucharist (First Communion)

The Eucharist is the source and center of Catholic life. Children typically prepare for their First Communion in second grade. We offer both English and Spanish-language programs.

Program details and dates: Contact us for information

Penance & Reconciliation

Through Penance, we receive God's mercy and are reconciled with the Church. Confession is available at regular times each week. Reconciliation is essential to living a full sacramental life.

Confession times: View Mass & Confession Schedule

Marriage

Marriage is a sacred covenant. St. Stephen provides marriage preparation, spiritual guidance, and support for engaged couples. We celebrate marriage as an essential ministry of the Church.

Engaged? Contact us at least 6 months before your wedding.

Holy Orders (Priesthood & Diaconate)

God calls men to serve as priests and permanent deacons. If you feel called to Holy Orders or simply want to learn more, our pastoral staff welcomes your questions.

Vocational counseling available: Speak with our pastor

Anointing of the Sick

The Anointing of the Sick brings spiritual healing and comfort to those facing serious illness. This sacrament is available to the homebound and hospitalized. Call our office to request a priest.

Need this sacrament? Contact us

Becoming Catholic (OCIA)

Are you interested in learning more about Catholicism or preparing to enter the Church? Our Order of Christian Initiation of Adults (OCIA) program welcomes inquirers, catechumens, and candidates.

Next session: Contact our office for dates and registration.

Have Questions?

Our pastoral staff is ready to help. Reach out with any questions about the sacraments or how to prepare.
